JUNE 30 2026

Summer is kickin' my booty a little. But I love it still. I love being sweaty and being able to smell things. I love having visitors come visit from home and getting to show them around Boston, it's a lot of my friends' and family's first time up here because they've never had a reason to come this far North. Got to see crazy whales in Gloucester with my grandma from OK and that was dope. Also went to Wollaston Beach with my dad on Father's Day. A storm came in over the city and the harbors in the distance when we were sitting there and it was very beautiful. Work got rough and I was out for the count for like a week or two, whatever. I have been spending a lot of my free time doing websites... and not sure if I like that lol. I transfered the neocities stuff to be more "legit" on github and local files and splish. I think the majority of my time goes to setting up things for the future. And making lists. I'm reading "The Iceman Cometh" right now and it's basically all that besides the heavy drinking, except my pipe dream will blow up >:).
Hammers sitting still has got that a show on July 12th at the Lilypad PLUS a 2 hour Gig at a bar that'll be mostly covers on July 26th. Compiling a list of songs to play for that so that we can sit and learn them later...
Yesterday I found this George Jones cover of The Blues Man by HWII with a Dolly appearance and cried a lot.
I led an open mic at Sennott park for Camberville Folk on Sunday with big ol lip from a mouth surgery on Fri. I met some cool people and got to hear some cool songs.
I almost wrote a new song. It's got a fast strum part :0 it takes me so long to write 1 song it's embarrassing.
Never investigated This Terry Allen album til last week. Wow! I grew up in Lubbock as a kid and much of it lives in me still. I love how much he tries to hate it but you can tell he doesn't.
This Boston band Pew Pew put out a new album a few weeks ago that I've been listening to a TON.
I hope if anyone reads this, that they are having a good time and June.

Woah just updated the Hammers Sitting still site again (w it's own updates page!) cuz we got two random shows to fill out the whole summer :D Rock n roll show at The Silhouette Lounge on may 27th. The sil was the first venue i went to to see a show when we moved here last summer so that's cool. and our buddies Finger Foods from nashville are coming july 12th at the lilypad which will be dope too. Used to play w them with when they'd come thru Knoxville. Feel very blessed that these shows popped up for us within just a couple days, as it's a good push towards figuring out what this project will be for Kozbi and I and fulfills a continuing goal of having a show per month. Lets go. We saw Hum this wknd in nyc it was epic. and we stayed with our friend Ted who makes music under Law Office.

While this isn't a true "newsletter" as there's no emails involved (maybe there will be one day), I still kinda wanna use this page within the hammers sitting still website (for now) to write about whatever interesting things that've been going on/ I've been consuming w perhaps helpful links. May use this page as an overall updater to other projects of mine in the future as well. whatever. I found out about Car vs Driver this week and it makes me happy. You can tell it's real emotional writing while still not comprising dope riffs and arrangements. and it's a fire band name. I also learned about Jacob King this week via some bandcamp journey and the force on this is rippin. perfect use of reverb in a live setting and I'm a sucker for live recordings of any kind really. As an instagram fiend I keep noticing this Age of Peace album This is the direction of folk I've been directing my little folk walking shoes towards for a little bit. They sound so free and in love. Seems like I need bigger shoes and a wider stride to keep up. or maybe just guitar lessons. Speaking of guitar lessons, I found this website for Sam Davis via Google maps around Cambridge looking for guitar stores. Man it's so cool. His Flyers are so awesome and funny makes me miss the days when physical posters were a more used format for local advertisement. I've been trying to get deeper in the flyer design world and it is a mysterious place. forces me to refine my design processes. For the first time I designed a poster almost fully in my head while in bed in the morning and made it within like 10 minutes when I got up. Maybe that's a normal thing for people, it usually takes me while of messing around. Lmk if you, reader, want a poster or anything. I love the designs this person from Chciago (I think?) makes. Maybe I'll run into them one day and we can talk solid color backgrounds and superimposition. I also love this dude Aidan from Athens' designs. He plays in a bunch of dope Athens bands. Inspiring me to try and do more hand-involved splish. Maybe move away from the photo digital collage style I usually am used to. Thanks modern architecture school.

There's some words for you. I sense good things ahead this summer.
